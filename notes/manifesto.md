# The Why

Here it is. The start. It is a pleasure to be here. 
By the end of HTMElephant, I was a real developer. Or at least the closest i've ever felt to being one. 

I think I have a nack for learning new things. I can pick things up quickly. I have found an interesting thing about myself. The things most people tend to "show interest" in tend to fade quickly and they just end up "doing it" because that's what they know. 

I feel as though I do things because I enjoy doing them. And even on the days I feel like I am doing it because I "have to", I still really enjoy "having" to do something. There is a certain sense of accomplishment from doing things even when you don't want to. That's almost more rewarding than doing the same things when you DO want to. 

I loved tinting cars. Even the hard cars. It was a challenge. It called for the best of me both mentally and physically. I tinted with several other tinters along the way and a lot of them seem to have fallen out love with the idea of tinting a car. I can confidently say that while tinting, I always wanted to tint. Even now that I take the occasional side job, I miss tinting every day. I never got sick of it. I always found a way to enjoy what I was doing. 

I enjoy working with my hands. A lot. I don't mind being a service advisor, but it is not where I want to be for the long term. Web development is a career. A new kind of work that can give me more time to do the things I love and grow my family. 

I can see myself thriving at a company where I can work hard all day, achieve goals, feel good, and then go home to my wife and future kids. 
I am writing this today as a devout husband and hoping to soon be a father. My wife and I would love nothing more than to have a child right now. 

Web development will bring out the best and the worst in me. I know full well that it is not going to be a cake walk, but I've never really enjoyed the easy way out. I do however enjoy working towards something and I think achieving a job in web development will keep me on an upward trend for decades to come.
